• Walt Disney •

The man who inspired millions of people

Here's a time line of Walt Disney's life:

  • 1901 - Walt Disney is born - Walt Disney was born to Elias and Flora Disney in Chicago.
  • 1910 - Life on the farm - When Walt was a child, the Disney family relocated to a farm. He loved to draw and imagine, but when his father became ill, they had to leave the farm and relocate to Kansas City, Missouri.
  • 1911 - Walt discovers movies - Walt got a paper route while residing in Missouri to help support the family financially. He first discovered movies and fell in love with them while working at this job.
  • 1917 - Art school - Walt proceeded drawing, and when his family returned to Chicago he enlisted within the Chicago Foundation of Fine Expressions. It was his dream to work as a daily paper cartoonist.
  • 1918 - World War I - In spite of the fact that he was too young to join the army, Walt lied around his age in order to enroll. He battled in France for a year within the American Ambulance Corps.
  • 1920-23 - Walt's failure - Walt joined forces with Ub Iwerks and formed a commercial craftsman company. However, the company failed after only a month. Over a number of years, Walt attempted a few different wanders which failed, including the Laugh-O-gram Organization. He eventually established Disney Brothers Studio in Hollywood, which would afterward become Hyperion Studio.
  • 1928 - Mickey Mouse is created - Walt produced "Steamboat Willie" during the height of the talking-picture revolution. It was an instant smash since it featured a talking mouse named Mickey.
  • 1930 - Mickey Mouse merchandising - Walt and his brother Roy planned to capitalize on Mickey's increasing popularity. They launched a merchandise line and the Mickey Mouse Club, which grew to over one million members in no time.
  • 1937 - Disney's first full length film - After making a few "short" movies, Walt chose to move ahead with full-length movies. Snow White and the Seven Midgets was released.
  • 1942 - World War II - After Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs, Walt Disney released multiple films including Fantasia, Pinocchio, and Dumbo. Even though the US Army took over part of the studio to house troops when WWII broke out, Disney was still able to film Bambi.
  • 1950 - Cinderella is a hit - Disney released Cinderella, and it was a hit. It was their greatest success since the discharge of Snow White.
  • 1955 - Disneyland opens - Walt Disney acquired land in California and built the Disneyland theme park. They had over one million guests in only two months
  • 1965 - Disney World ideas begin - Walt bought land in Florida to construct "Epcot", a theme park that would showcase "tomorrow's" technologies. It was the seed of what would become Disney World.
  • 1966 - Walt Disney Dies - At the age of 66, Walt Disney passed away due to lung cancer on December 15, 1966. Within the same year, Winnie the Pooh and the Honey Tree released, and Walt's legacy proceeded with a handful of movies and different Disney theme parks around the world.
